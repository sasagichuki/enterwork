RED RALLY: THE RACE TO ROAD SAFETY

With 3,800 tragic road accidents in 2012 and 1,823 in 2013, the Kenya Red Cross was tired of all the senseless injuries and fatalities that were taking place, that could be avoided. With the festive season round the corner, they knew they had to step in and they did, with an exciting gamified campaign that used popular social media tools to show how even the fastest drivers play it safe on the roads.

The Problem

Every year thousands of Kenyans lose their lives as a result of road carnage. This is especially so during the festive season when people travel to see their loved ones.

Over the years, research has shown that most lives are lost due to reckless driving. The Kenya Red Cross Society decided something needed to be done to educate people on the importance of road safety, especially safe driving.

The Insight

People love gamified experiences that have attainable goals and give them a competitive challenge.

Nowadays more and more people can be found on their mobile phones or online, playing games.

Our Idea

We got fans behind the wheel of two of the fastest rally drivers. Through tweets, shares and likes, they fuelled their favourite driver to the finish line.

As they raced, they learnt and shared the different hazards that were on the road and the precautions that they should take that would make this festive season safe for all.

How we did it

  • Step 1

    Fans would spot social media posts inviting them to take on the rally challenge.

  • Step 2

    They would visit our Red Rally microsite to play the game.

  • Step 3

    To kick-start the challenge, they would choose one of the two rally drivers.

  • Step 4

    To speed up their favourite rally driver, they would like, share, and retweet the road safety messages.
